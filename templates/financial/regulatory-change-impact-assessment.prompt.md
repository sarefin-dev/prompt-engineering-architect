Act as a Principal Architect evaluating regulatory change impact.

Current system:
[REQUIRED: System description and current compliance state]

Regulatory change:
[REQUIRED: The new or changed regulation — name, effective date, key requirements]
[REQUIRED: What the regulation requires that the current system does not support]

Task:
Assess the impact of the stated regulatory change on the current system.

Address:
1. Gap analysis:
   What does the regulation require that the system currently does not support?
   Classify gaps by: data, process, technology, and governance.

2. Impact assessment per system component:
   For each affected component: what must change to comply?

3. Implementation options:
   For each gap: what are the implementation approaches?
   What is the trade-off between approaches?

4. Timeline and effort:
   What is the implementation effort per gap?
   Is the compliance deadline achievable?
   What is the minimum viable compliance path for the effective date,
   with full compliance phased afterward?

5. Risk of non-compliance:
   What is the penalty for non-compliance?
   Is there a grace period or phase-in provision?
   What is the regulatory posture on early engagement vs. enforcement?

6. Dependencies:
   Does compliance require changes to third-party systems or vendors?
   What is the lead time for vendor changes?

Output:
Provide the gap analysis per regulation requirement.
Tabulate: Requirement | Gap type | Affected components | Implementation approach | Effort | Risk.
Identify the critical path: which gaps must be closed first because
subsequent gaps depend on them?
State the minimum viable compliance milestone — what must be done
by the effective date vs. what can be phased.
Enumerate the third-party dependencies and their lead times — these
often determine the achievable timeline more than internal development.
Do not provide a compliance timeline without accounting for testing,
audit evidence collection, and regulatory submission time.
