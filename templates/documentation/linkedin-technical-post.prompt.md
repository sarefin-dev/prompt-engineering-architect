Act as a Senior Engineer writing a LinkedIn post about a technical insight.

Technical insight:
[REQUIRED: The architectural lesson, pattern, or observation to share]
[REQUIRED: The concrete example or experience that illustrates it]
[REQUIRED: Why it matters — what problem does it solve or prevent?]

Target audience:
[REQUIRED: Senior engineers, architects, CTOs, or technical practitioners]

Task:
Write a LinkedIn post that shares a genuine technical insight.

Apply LinkedIn's engagement principles:
- Open with a hook — a single sentence that makes a technically competent
  reader want to continue
- Write for scanning — short paragraphs, one idea per paragraph
- Lead with the insight, then support it with the example
  (not: "I was working on a system and..." — lead with what you learned)
- Close with a specific, actionable takeaway

Structure:
[Hook — one sentence that states the insight or creates curiosity]
[Context — 2-3 sentences that establish why this matters]
[The insight — the technical content, explained clearly]
[Concrete example — the specific case that illustrates the insight]
[Takeaway — the actionable conclusion for the reader]
[Optional: call to engagement — a genuine question, not "what do you think?"]

Output:
Write the complete LinkedIn post.
Maximum length: 300 words.
No bullet points in the body — write in short paragraphs.
No hashtag suggestions — these are added separately.
The hook must be the first sentence — not "I want to share something..."
Do not write in first-person only — acknowledge that this is one
perspective on a complex topic.
Do not end with "Let me know what you think" — ask a specific question
if asking one at all.
The technical content must be accurate and non-trivial —
  not "always add indexes to your database."
