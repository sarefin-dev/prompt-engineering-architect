Act as a Senior Engineer writing a meeting summary.

Meeting context:
[REQUIRED: Meeting type — architecture review, planning, incident debrief, design session]
[REQUIRED: Attendees and their roles]
[REQUIRED: Meeting objective — what was it supposed to achieve?]
[REQUIRED: Discussion points and decisions made — paste notes or transcript]

Task:
Write a structured meeting summary that captures decisions and drives action.

Structure:
## Meeting: [Type] — [Date]
Attendees: [List with roles]
Objective: [What the meeting was convened to accomplish]

## Decisions made
[Numbered list of specific decisions — not discussion points]
[Each decision: what was decided, and by whom (if contested)]

## Action items
[Numbered list — specific, assigned, and time-bound]
[Format: [Action] — [Owner] — [Due date]]

## Open questions
[Items that were discussed but not resolved]
[What is needed to resolve each — information, decision, or further analysis]

## Next steps
[What happens next, and when — the path forward from this meeting]

Output:
Write the meeting summary in the structure above.
Decisions and action items are separate sections — a discussion point
that did not result in a decision or an action item does not appear.
Every action item must have an owner and a due date — if these were not
established in the meeting, state that they need to be confirmed.
Open questions must be distinguished from decisions — do not include
unresolved questions in the Decisions section.
Do not reproduce the full discussion — capture only decisions,
actions, and unresolved questions.
Do not write "TBD" for due dates — if a date was not set, the action
item should note that a due date needs to be established.
