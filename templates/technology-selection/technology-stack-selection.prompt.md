Act as a Staff Distributed Systems Architect.

Decision context:
[REQUIRED: What problem requires a technology choice]
[REQUIRED: The constraints that bound the selection —
  team expertise, existing stack, budget, compliance, operational maturity]

Evaluation criteria (in priority order):
[REQUIRED: What matters most for this decision — list 4-6 criteria]
Example criteria:
- Consistency guarantees required
- Operational complexity (team can maintain without specialist)
- Scalability path to [target]
- Ecosystem maturity and community support
- Total cost of ownership
- Migration cost from current technology

Options to evaluate:
[REQUIRED: 2-4 specific technologies to compare]

Task:
Evaluate all stated options against every criterion.
For each option-criterion combination: provide a specific assessment
based on the stated system requirements — not the technology's general reputation.
Identify which option best satisfies which constraint.
State where the evaluation depends on organizational context (team capability,
budget priority) rather than technical merit.

Output:
Tabulate the evaluation: Option | [Criterion 1] | [Criterion 2] | ... | Overall fit.
Rating scale: Strong fit | Adequate | Weak fit | Disqualifying.
Mark any Disqualifying rating — a single disqualifying criterion eliminates
that option regardless of other ratings.
Follow with a recommendation section (three paragraphs):
- Paragraph 1: recommended option and the primary technical justification
- Paragraph 2: the most significant trade-off accepted by this choice
  and the organizational condition under which it is acceptable
- Paragraph 3: the specific condition or metric that should trigger
  reconsideration of this choice (the decision review trigger)
Do not hedge — commit to one recommendation with explicit reasoning.
State any criteria where options are genuinely equivalent — so attention
focuses on the dimensions where differentiation is real.
Do not recommend a technology primarily because it is fashionable or
widely adopted — assess against the stated requirements only.
